     Amateur Radio Channel Naming Convention for Emergency Use
                 draft-kj5djc-ham-channels-01


Abstract

   This document defines a naming convention for amateur radio channels
   and high-frequency (HF) nets used in emergency and public service
   communications. The convention provides a consistent and scalable
   structure that avoids conflicts with National Interoperability Field
   Operations Guide (NIFOG) channel names while remaining simple to use
   under operational conditions.

1.  Introduction

   Amateur radio operators supporting emergency communications require
   a clear and consistent method for identifying communication
   resources. Public safety interoperability channels defined in NIFOG
   (e.g., VCALL, VTAC) are reserved for interagency use and MUST NOT be
   repurposed within amateur radio channel plans.

   This document defines a naming convention intended for use by amateur
   radio organizations.

2.  Conventions and Terminology

   The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "SHOULD", and "MAY" in this document
   are to be interpreted as described in BCP 14 (RFC 2119 and RFC 8174).

3.  Naming Structure

      [Band][Function][Number][Suffix]

4.  Naming Components

4.1.  Band Designator

   The Band field identifies the operating band and MUST be one of:

      2     2 meter (VHF)
      70    70 centimeter (UHF)
      9     33 centimeter (902–928 MHz)
      80    80 meter (HF)
      40    40 meter (HF)
      20    20 meter (HF) (optional)

4.2.  Function

      CALL  Calling, hailing, or coordination
      HAM   Tactical or working communications

4.3.  Number

      10    Primary channel
      11    Secondary channel
      12+   Additional channels

4.4.  Suffix

      (none)  Voice (default)
      R       Repeater (analog FM)
      D       Digital (e.g., DMR, JS8, Winlink)

5.  VHF and UHF Channel Definitions

5.1.  2 Meter (VHF)

      2CALL10
      2HAM10
      2HAM10R
      2HAM10D

5.2.  70 Centimeter (UHF)

      70CALL10
      70HAM10
      70HAM10R
      70HAM10D

5.3.  33 Centimeter (900 MHz)

      9CALL10
      9HAM10
      9HAM10R
      9HAM10D

6.  HF Net Definitions

      80CALL10
      80HAM10
      80HAM10D

      40CALL10
      40HAM10
      40HAM10D

7.  Channel Creation Guidelines

   Channels MUST follow the defined structure and component rules.

8.  Operational Usage

   CALL channels MUST be used for coordination.
   HAM channels MUST be used for tactical operations.

9.  Interoperability Considerations

   NIFOG channel names MUST NOT be reused.

10.  Radio Programming Considerations

   Channels MUST map to frequency, tone, and mode.

11.  Security Considerations

   Amateur communications are non-secure.

12.  IANA Considerations

   None.
